# Content System — The SHIP Method OS Growth Track

**Purpose:** A repeatable content engine for building the audience that feeds the SHIP Builder List (see `LANDING_PAGE_TH.md` for the opt-in page and `EMAIL_SEQUENCE.md` for what happens after signup). This is lead generation for the paid SHIP Method OS repo — every piece of content either teaches a real SHIP concept or shows proof it works, and every piece points back to one CTA: join the list.

**Non-negotiable rule:** Free content teaches the *thinking* (the "why" and "what" of Structure/Human Flow/Instruction/Publish). It never gives away the *system* (the actual templates, SOPs, and prompt libraries) — that's the paid repo. If a post could replace someone's reason to buy the OS, cut it down until it's a taste, not a meal.

**Platforms covered:** TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, X (Twitter), Threads, YouTube.

---

## 1. Hook Templates (10)

Use these as fill-in-the-blank openers for any platform. Brackets are placeholders — replace with specifics from a real build, persona, or failure mode. Don't soften them; the bracket content should be embarrassingly specific.

1. "Most people think [building with AI is about better prompts], but actually [it's about skipping a step nobody told you existed]."
2. "I watched [a non-technical founder] spend [3 weeks] rebuilding the same feature [4 times] — here's the 1 thing that would've stopped it."
3. "Nobody tells you this about [AI-built products]: [the AI isn't the bottleneck, your missing spec is]."
4. "[90%] of AI builds fail for the same dumb reason: [they skip Structure and jump straight to code]."
5. "I asked [ChatGPT/Claude/Cursor] to build [a CRM] with zero spec. Here's what it built vs. what I actually wanted."
6. "If your AI-built app keeps [breaking / contradicting itself / scope-creeping], it's not a prompting problem — it's a [missing Human Flow] problem."
7. "Before you open another AI chat to build [your product], answer these [4 questions] — or AI will guess for you."
8. "Here's the exact [5-minute] thing I do before every AI build session that [90% of builders] skip."
9. "[Idea] to [shipped product] in [a weekend] — here's the order of operations, step by step."
10. "Stop saying 'build me an app that does X.' Say this instead: [the one-sentence reframe that changes AI's output]."

## 2. Content Ideas Bank (20)

Grouped by SHIP phase first, then tagged by content type so you can cross-reference for variety. Each idea is specific enough to outline in 10 minutes.

### Structure (S)

1. **[Educational]** "The 5 questions you must answer before opening any AI chat" — walk through who/what/why/scope/done, using a fake product as the example.
2. **[Hot take]** "Your problem isn't AI — it's that you don't know what you're building yet." Call out the "build me an app" prompt pattern directly.
3. **[Case study]** Before/after: a builder's vague 1-sentence idea vs. the same idea after 15 minutes of structuring (tease the SHIP Canvas, see `SHIP_CANVAS.md`).
4. **[Tutorial]** "How to write a one-paragraph product vision that actually constrains AI's output" — live demo, screen-recorded.
5. **[Behind-the-scenes]** Show your own messy first draft of a PROJECT.md-style doc, then the cleaned-up version. Make the mess relatable.

### Human Flow (H)

6. **[Educational]** "Why you should design the user journey before writing a single AI prompt" — 3 real consequences of skipping this.
7. **[Hot take]** "Wireframes aren't for designers anymore. They're how you control what AI builds." Reframe wireframing as an AI-control tool.
8. **[Tutorial]** "Map a 3-screen user journey in 10 minutes using just bullet points" — no design tool needed, paper-napkin method.
9. **[Case study]** A directory site that failed its first AI build because the builder never mapped what happens when a search returns zero results — then the fix.
10. **[Behind-the-scenes]** Screen-record yourself sketching information architecture for a real (anonymized) product idea.

### Instruction (I)

11. **[Educational]** "AI is a literal, fast builder — not a mind reader. Here's the difference that matters." Explain why specificity beats cleverness.
12. **[Tutorial]** "Turn a user flow into an AI-ready build prompt" — show a bad prompt next to a good one, side by side.
13. **[Hot take]** "Most 'prompt engineering' content is solving the wrong problem. The bottleneck is missing structure, not phrasing."
14. **[Case study]** Same feature request, two outcomes: one builder gives AI a 2-line prompt, another gives it a full spec. Show both outputs.
15. **[Behind-the-scenes]** "Here's the actual prompt chain I use to go from spec to working code" (tease the structure, don't give the full library — that's the paid repo).

### Publish (P)

16. **[Educational]** "Shipping isn't the finish line — it's where the real feedback loop starts." Explain why most builders stop right before the useful part.
17. **[Tutorial]** "5-minute QA pass before you ship anything AI built you" — a checklist walkthrough (high-level, not the full repo checklist).
18. **[Case study]** A solo builder who shipped V1 in a weekend using SHIP, what broke in week one, and how the feedback loop caught it fast.
19. **[Hot take]** "Your AI-built MVP doesn't need to be perfect. It needs a working feedback loop. Here's the difference."
20. **[Behind-the-scenes]** Screen-record a real launch day — analytics setup, first user feedback coming in, what you triage first.

## 4. CTA Templates (8)

All CTAs drive to the SHIP Builder List signup (the landing page in `LANDING_PAGE_TH.md`). Match the format to platform norms — TikTok/Instagram can't do clickable links in posts, so route to bio; LinkedIn/X/Facebook/YouTube descriptions can carry a direct link.

1. **TikTok / Instagram (link-in-bio):** "Free SHIP Canvas — turn your idea into a real plan in 15 minutes. Link in bio."
2. **TikTok / Instagram (link-in-bio, urgency variant):** "Stop guessing what to build. Grab the free worksheet in my bio before your next AI session."
3. **LinkedIn (direct link, professional tone):** "I put the full breakdown into a free one-page worksheet. Link in the comments — no opt-in tricks, just the canvas."
4. **LinkedIn (direct link, comment-bait variant):** "Comment 'SHIP' and I'll send you the free canvas directly."
5. **X / Twitter (direct link, thread closer):** "If this thread was useful, the free worksheet version is here: [link]. RT if it'll help someone else building right now."
6. **Threads (link-in-bio, casual tone):** "Free worksheet for this — drop it in your bio link, go fill it out before you open AI again."
7. **YouTube (description direct link + verbal CTA):** "Full canvas + checklist linked in the description below — it's free, no credit card, just an email to send you the next one."
8. **Facebook (direct link, community tone):** "Grab the free SHIP Canvas here: [link] — built this from watching the same mistake happen over and over. Tag a friend who's stuck on their AI build."
